Track Build

Decide your class and use-case first: tires, pads, cooling, and safety gear beat raw power. Start with a square wheel/tire setup, quality pads/fluids, and fresh bushings. Keep ride height conservative, add camber, and prioritize reliability over cosmetics.

Starter list: track-capable brake pads & fluid, stainless lines, alignment (-2.0° to -2.5° camber front), performance tires, and a proper inspection checklist.

Street Build

For a daily you’ll love, keep comfort and NVH in mind. Mild drop or quality coilovers, OEM+ wheel fitment, and responsive brakes. Tune for drivability and heat management, keep emissions-compliant, and choose tires that balance grip with longevity.

Starter list: mild coilovers or springs, alignment (-1.0° to -1.5° camber front), performance all-season tires, and a quiet, efficient exhaust system.

Finding Reliable Shops

Read recent reviews, ask for alignment sheets and before/after photos, and confirm insurance. Good shops communicate lead times, torque specs, and test drive the car. Get quotes in writing.

Starter tips: bring your parts list, torque values, and alignment targets to the consult. A good shop will help you plan for reliability and performance.

Doing a Shakedown

Before big parts or power, shake the car down: fluids, leaks, temps, brake feel, tire rub, and noises. Log temps, do progressive pulls, and re-torque wheels and key suspension hardware. Adjust alignment/ride height after baseline testing, not before.

Starter tips: check for consistent brake feel, tire wear, and fluid levels after each session. A proper shakedown ensures reliability before pushing limits.

Tweaking the Build

Once the basics are sorted, fine-tune your build for your specific goals. Tires, brakes, and suspension geometry are key areas to focus on. Test and adjust incrementally to find the perfect balance.

Starter tips: experiment with tire pressures, brake pad compounds, and damper settings. Keep detailed logs of changes and results to track progress and improvements.
